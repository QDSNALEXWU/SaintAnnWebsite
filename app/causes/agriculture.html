Food and Agriculture .

Description

With the right start, children can grow into healthy adults able to feed themselves. Children who don’t eat enough can’t grow, feel, excel in school or live an active, healthy life. In the communities we serve we grow food to feed the children, who due to their low-income potentials are unable to grow enough food to feed their own families, much less sell for additional income. That is why we partner with these communities to address their immediate food needs. We work with volunteers to increase agricultural productivity through improved seeds and farming practices, improve access to markets so farmers can profitably sell their surplus food while integrating projects that often combine vegetable gardening, livestock management and nutrition education.
